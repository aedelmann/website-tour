Picture this: we roll into **Madrid** for the very first time after a proper Odyssey-mode drive in the Tesla—all the way from the **Netherlands**. Legs half asleep, playlist fully depleted, and the car looking at us like, “cool, we made it, please never ask me to do that again without a spa day.”

And Madrid’s welcome gift? **37 degrees.** Not “warm.” Not “oh a bit sunny.” The kind of heat that makes asphalt feel personal. We stepped out, got hit by a wall of hot air, and immediately understood why every Spaniard within a 5 km radius was moving at the speed of a chilled cortado.

## The drive: Netherlands → Madrid (brain: buffering)

Coming from the Netherlands, Madrid feels like teleporting into a different climate dimension. One minute you’re politely sharing road with bikes; the next you’re navigating a capital that flexes **Beaux-Arts drama**, statues on rooftops, and boulevards wide enough to host a small parade of overheating tourists (us).

By the time we hit Gran Vía energy—ornate facades, iron balconies, that Metropolis dome doing its winged-statue main-character thing—we were grinning like people who had earned every single café stop.

## Heat mode: activated

**37°C** in the city is not a vibe, it is a lifestyle choice the weather made without consulting us. Sharp shadows, bright stone, and the official Madrid sport of darting between shade patches like you’re speedrunning Minecraft.

Pro tip from the melted: hydrate, slow down, and accept that your “quick stroll” will become a full cardio event with architectural bonus points.

## Architecture, cafés, and the art of not going broke

Madrid does **great architecture**—the kind that makes you look up until your neck files a formal complaint. Domes, columns, royal-palace grandeur, street canyons of cream-stone façades. It feels big, stylish, and slightly smug about it (deserved).

Then the recovery loop: **nice cafés and restaurants**. Sit down, order something cold, pretend you’re locals who always look this composed. Spoiler: we do not.

And here’s the plot twist northern Europe never prepares you for—Madrid is **not too expensive**. You can eat well, wander hard, and still leave with a wallet that has not entered witness protection. After Dutch/German price trauma, the bill occasionally feels like a typo in our favor.

## Hotel tip for Tesla humans: Hotel 101 near the airport

If you’re rolling in by car (especially electric), save yourself the downtown parking soap opera and stay at **Hotel 101** near the airport. It’s **good, cheap**, and—chef’s kiss for us—**includes charging for cars in the free car park**.

That combo is rare enough that we almost wrote them a thank-you note. Almost. The heat melted our stationery motivation.

Arrive drained from a long European haul, plug in, sleep, wake up with range anxiety fully exorcised. Highly recommend.

## Tomorrow’s plot: Málaga, then home to Jerez

This Madrid chapter is a spicy little interlude. **Tomorrow we’re heading out to Málaga**, then back to **Jerez** (home base, sofa, and the fridge that knows our names).

Madrid, you were hot—literally—and somehow still charming. First visit unlocked. Architecture admired. Cafés conquered. Tesla fed. Dignity… mostly intact.

## Key takeaways

1. **First Madrid hit:** Worth the long Tesla run from the Netherlands—arrive ready to gawk upward.
2. **Heat reality:** ~37°C means shade is a personality trait. Schedule outdoor flex for earlier/later hours.
3. **City vibes:** Big architecture + solid cafés/restaurants + prices that won’t vaporize your budget.
4. **Stay smart:** **Hotel 101** near the airport—affordable, solid, free parking, and car charging included.
5. **Next up:** Málaga stop, then home to Jerez. The road trip continues; the AC stays non-negotiable.

*Hasta pronto, Madrid—you had us at “look at that dome” and kept us with “wait, the bill is how much?”*
